# Ethical Guidelines for AI-Driven Retention

## Foundation of Automated Systems
As we increasingly transition toward machine learning and predictive AI to manage our customer base, it is imperative that our strategies remain firmly rooted in ethical business practices. The deployment of complex algorithms to analyze behavioral data and predict flight risk presents significant regulatory and moral challenges. This document serves as the binding operational framework for all AI-augmented retention efforts, ensuring we preserve customer trust, maintain strict demographic equity, and ensure total operational transparency.

## Mandatory Human Oversight
Artificial intelligence is a powerful analytical tool, but it lacks the contextual empathy necessary for final customer engagement. Therefore, **all AI-generated retention recommendations must be reviewed by a qualified human agent before customer contact is initiated**. Algorithms may prioritize accounts and generate optimal scripts or discount frameworks, but a human must independently verify the appropriateness of the intervention before opening a ticket to contact the account holder.

## Prohibition of Psychological Manipulation
Marketing algorithms often default to urgency tactics to drive conversion, but manufactured scarcity damages long-term brand equity. Agents and automated marketing bots must **never use urgency manipulation** (e.g., falsely stating "your account will be cancelled" or "this offer expires in 1 hour") without a strictly documented factual basis in the billing system.

## Transparency and Customer Consent
We operate under a paradigm of full disclosure regarding our use of analytics. **Customers must be informed if they are participating in an AI-driven retention program upon their request**. If a customer inquires how their account was flagged for a specific discount or outreach call, agents are required to transparently state that their historical utilization patterns were flagged by our automated systems. Furthermore, **customers retain the absolute right to opt out of automated retention programs entirely**, requiring a manual toggle in the CRM to exclude their data from future modeling.

## Strict Non-Discrimination Policies
Our retention pricing models must be completely detached from socioeconomic and demographic biases. When algorithms prescribe promotional offers or loyalty rewards, **discount offers must be equally available to all customers falling within the same risk tier**. There is a strict zero-tolerance policy for discriminatory targeting based on protected characteristics. 
To technically enforce this, **data used for churn prediction must not include race, religion, gender, or national origin as direct model features**. Engineering teams are tasked with rigorously auditing training datasets to strip these columns and evaluating output for latent proxy bias.

## System Disclaimers
To reinforce internal accountability, the AI infrastructure itself must constantly remind users of its limitations. All internal software interfaces displaying algorithmic recommendations to agents must prominently include the following mandated disclaimer: **"Generated by AI — human review recommended before implementation."** This reminds the workforce that the AI is an assistant, not an autonomous authority.

# Contract Retention Playbook

## Executive Summary
This playbook serves as a definitive guide for tier-1 and tier-2 retention agents when engaging with at-risk telecom customers. Because contract structures heavily dictate an account's probability of cancellation, our retention workflows must dynamically adapt based on a customer's specific contract terms, tenure, and billing circumstances. Adhering strictly to these decision matrices ensures we maximize the lifetime value of the customer while minimizing unnecessary margin degradation. All offers should be positioned as exclusive account reviews.

## High-Risk Decision Table Matrix
To systematize our retention efforts, agents must follow prescribed logic regarding offer deployment. The following decision table format dictates the priority level and corresponding necessary action based on account parameters.

### Month-to-Month Early Lifecycle
**Condition:** IF contract = Month-to-month AND tenure < 6 months  
**Priority:** CRITICAL  
**Action:** Same-day intervention call required. The agent is authorized to provide a **20% discount offer** contingent on a 12-month contract upgrade. This segment possesses the highest flight risk as they have not yet established loyalty and have minimal switching costs.

### Month-to-Month Late Lifecycle / High Value
**Condition:** IF contract = Month-to-month AND tenure is between 6 and 24 months AND monthly charges > $65  
**Priority:** HIGH  
**Action:** Initiate a personalized email campaign followed by a phone intervention. Provide a high-value loyalty reward (such as a complimentary premium service add-on) coupled with a standard contract upgrade offer. This group generates strong revenue but remains entirely unsecured.

### Annual Contract Maturity / High Value
**Condition:** IF contract = One year AND tenure > 24 months AND monthly charges > $80  
**Priority:** MEDIUM  
**Action:** Provide a straightforward Two-year upgrade offer featuring locked-in pricing. The messaging should emphasize financial predictability and protection against future inflation or rate hikes, securing long-term revenue from an already established, highly profitable customer base.

## Secondary Escalation Rules
A single rejection does not signify a lost save opportunity. If the customer rejects the first contact retention offer, the account must be scheduled for a secondary campaign. Agents should **wait exactly 5 days**, allowing the customer a cooling-off period to consider their alternatives. Following this window, agents are authorized to offer an **additional 5% sequential discount** on top of the original retention offer. Pushing this secondary offer too quickly appears overly aggressive, whereas waiting longer than 5 days typically results in the customer finalizing competitor arrangements. 

## Win-Back Window Optimization
In scenarios where proactive retention fails and the account proceeds to cancellation status, immediate re-marketing efforts must activate. Our internal historical analyses dictate that the immediate post-cancellation period represents a highly viable recovery window. Specifically, **60% of churned customers who originally received no specialized retention offer** during their cancellation process can successfully be won back within a strict **30-day window**. Achieving this high win-back conversion necessitates a targeted **25% welcome-back discount**. Waiting beyond the 30-day threshold drops conversion rates to single digits as customers finalize installations with competing providers.
